Blurred Charcoal Drawing

Drawing I - 2017

This project was unlike anything I'd done before. We were first shown a very blurred image on the projector and were told to draw it. Every 35 minutes the image became a little less blurred. After 5, 35 minutes sessions, we finally got to see the fully visible image...

It was a black and white, upside-down version of one of my favorite paintings — Judith Slaying Holofernes by Artemisia Gentileschi. This assignment was to teach us how to see light and shadow. It was to show us that we need to focus on large, general shapes at first, rather than get stuck working on the little details.
